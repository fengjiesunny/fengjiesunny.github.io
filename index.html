About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Agricultural and Applied Economics at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. Leveraging my personal experience in the genomics industry, my research focuses on topics related to agricultural and health economics, including pricing analysis under demand complementarity, demand analysis for U.S. corn and soybean seeds, and aging and rural development in China. I also participated in several innovative research projects: including the UMETRICS project; 2xcelerate (A diploid inbred line strategy to accelerate genetic gain in potato (USDA-AFRI)) project. As an economic educator, I am also passionate about delivering the applied economic concepts and methods to academic audiences and non-academic audiences.
